Overview

Contacts are individual people associated with customer accounts. One account (customer) can have multiple contacts, allowing you to track all decision-makers, influencers, and users at each company.

üîó Account-First Model

Contacts are always linked to an Account (Customer). You cannot create a contact without first having a customer account.

Key Features

üë• Multi-Person Support

  • Multiple Contacts: Add unlimited contacts per account
  • Primary Contact: Mark one contact as primary
  • Roles: Decision Maker, Influencer, User, Gatekeeper, Champion, Economic Buyer
  • Influence Score: High, Medium, Low
  • Preferred Channel: Email, WhatsApp, Phone, SMS, LinkedIn

How to Create a Contact

1 Navigate to Contacts Page

Click on "Contacts" in the sidebar navigation menu.

2 Click "Add Contact" Button

Click the "Add Contact" button at the top right.

3 Select Account

Important: First select the Account (Customer) this contact belongs to. You cannot create a contact without an account.

4 Fill in Contact Information

Complete the contact form:

  • Name: Contact's full name (required)
  • Job Title: Position/title (e.g., Manager, Director)
  • Role: Select role (Decision Maker, Influencer, User, etc.)
  • Email: Contact email address
  • Phone: Contact phone number
  • Influence Score: High, Medium, or Low
  • Preferred Channel: How they prefer to be contacted
  • Is Primary Contact: Check if this is the primary contact
5 Save Contact

Click "Save" to create the contact. The contact will be linked to the selected account.

Viewing Contacts

1 Contact List

The contacts page shows all contacts with:

  • Contact name
  • Job title
  • Account name
  • Email and phone
  • Role
  • Primary contact indicator
2 Filter by Account

Use the account filter to view contacts for a specific customer account.

3 Search Contacts

Search by name, email, phone, or job title to quickly find contacts.

Editing Contacts

1 Open Edit Form

Click the "Edit" button next to the contact you want to edit.

2 Update Information

Modify any fields. You can change the account if needed, but contacts should typically stay with their original account.

3 Save Changes

Click "Save" to update the contact information.

Primary Contact

üí° Primary Contact: Each account should have one primary contact. This is the main person you communicate with at that company. When you mark a contact as primary, any existing primary contact for that account will be automatically unmarked.

Contact Roles Explained

  • Decision Maker: Has authority to make purchasing decisions
  • Influencer: Influences the decision but doesn't make it
  • User: Will use the product/service
  • Gatekeeper: Controls access to decision makers
  • Champion: Internal advocate for your solution
  • Economic Buyer: Controls the budget

Tips & Best Practices

üí° Tip 1: Always create the customer account first, then add contacts to it.
üí° Tip 2: Mark the main decision maker as the primary contact.
üí° Tip 3: Use roles to identify who has decision-making authority.
üí° Tip 4: Set preferred channel to ensure you contact people the way they prefer.
üí° Tip 5: Track influence scores to prioritize your outreach efforts.
